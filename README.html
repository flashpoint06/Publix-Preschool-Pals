Yes, we'll touch on everything from "Installation" to "Troubleshooting." We only hope we have the information you're looking for to make the hours your child spends with Bixby and Kimmy and the rest as fun as they can be.

1. SUGGESTED MINIMUMS

  • Pentium II 266mhz processor
  • 4X CD-ROM drive
  • 64 MB RAM
  • 85 MB available on hard drive
  • Monitor with minimum 640 X 480 resolution and 16 bit color

  • G3 266mhz processor
  • 4X CD-ROM or DVD drive
  • 80 MB free RAM
  • 150 MB available on hard drive
  • System 8.5 or higher


2. INSTALLATION

Windows® 95/98/Me/NT/2000:

Publix Preschool Pals utilizes the AutoPlay function of Windows® 95/98/Me/NT/2000.

  1. Insert the Publix Preschool Pals CD into your CD-ROM drive.

  2. Click on the Yes button when the Publix Preschool Pals Installation dialog window appears.

  3. Follow the on-screen prompts to complete the installation.

If your system does not support AutoPlay, follow these steps to install Publix Preschool Pals:

  1. Insert the Publix Preschool Pals CD into your CD-ROM drive.

  2. Click on My Computer.

  3. Click on your CD drive.

  4. Double-click publix_preschool_pals.exe.

  5. Follow the prompts to complete the installation.

Macintosh®:

  1. Insert the Publix Preschool Pals CD into your CD-ROM/DVD drive. Double click the CD icon when it appears.

  2. Double-click the Publix Preschool Pals Installer icon in the open window to install the program.
  3. Follow the prompts to complete the installation.
  4. Click on the Quit button when the installation is complete.


3. UNINSTALLING THE PROGRAM

Windows® 95/98/Me/NT/2000:

To remove Publix Preschool Pals from your system, follow these steps to use the Uninstall feature of the Publix Preschool Pals game.

  1. Click on Start.

  2. Move cursor to Programs.

  3. Move cursor to Publix Preschool Pals.

  4. Click on Uninstall.

  5. Click on "Yes" to the question "This will remove Publix Preschool Pals from your system, do you want to continue?"

  6. Click on Close.

You may also remove Publix Preschool Pals from your system using the uninstall function of Windows® 95/98/Me/NT/2000. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. Click on Start.

  2. Move cursor to Settings.

  3. Click on Control Panel.

  4. Double click on Add/Remove Programs.

  5. Click on Publix Preschool Pals.

  6. Click on Add/Remove.

  7. Click on Yes to complete uninstall.

  8. Go to Windows® Explorer and delete the Publix Preschool Pals folder from your hard drive.

Macintosh®:

To remove Publix Preschool Pals from your system, drag the Publix Preschool Pals folder and the desktop icon to the trash.



4. RUNNING THE PROGRAM

Windows® 95/98/Me/NT/2000:

Follow these steps to start Publix Preschool Pals:

  1. Close all running Windows® applications.

  2. Click on Start.

  3. Move the cursor to Programs and then to Publix Preschool Pals.

  4. Click on the Publix Publix Preschool Pals icon.

Or click on the Publix Preschool Pals desktop icon after closing all running Windows® applications.

Macintosh®:

  1. Close all running applications.

  2. Open the Publix Preschool Pals Folder on your hard drive and double-click the Publix Preschool Pals icon.

Or click on the Publix Preschool Pals desktop icon after closing all running applications.



5. EXITING THE PROGRAM

To stop playing the game in progress, simply follow the in-game navigation. Exiting the game by some other means may prevent the game from saving your most recent progress.



6. TROUBLESHOOTING

Basic Troubleshooting for Windows®

If you experience problems in installing or running your Publix Preschool Pals software under Windows®, try taking the following steps before you contact Technical Support:

For Installation Problems: Check Condition of CD-ROM

Fingerprints, smudges, or scratches will cause a program to malfunction. Take the following steps to check for a dirty or damaged CD:

  1. Take a dry cloth and wipe off the clear side of the CD from the inside ring to the outside edge.
  2. Check the CD for deep scratches. If it is damaged, you may have to obtain a replacement disc by calling 1-866-236-6980.
  3. If the CD looks fine, try running the program again.

For Problems in Running the Program: Check Your System For Possible Conflicts

You will need to ensure that there are no other programs running in the background of Windows® which might interfere with the running of the Publix Preschool Pals program. Close any other applications.

Reset the Game

You can reset the game so that the Difficulty Level on all games is set to Level One and the Coin Bank is empty by following these steps:

  1. Locate the Publix Preschool Pals folder.
  2. Delete the "players" file from the folder. This may be listed as either "players" or "players.txt". By deleting the players file you will lose the player's names, all coins collected to date and the last difficulty levels attained for each game.

If any problems persist, you will need to contact Technical Support at 1-866-236-6980.

Basic Troubleshooting for the Macintosh®

If you experience problems in installing or running your Publix Preschool Pals software on a Macintosh®, try taking the following steps before you contact Technical Support:

For Installation Problems: Check Condition of CD-ROM

Fingerprints, smudges, or scratches will cause a program to malfunction. Take the following steps to check for a dirty or damaged CD:

  1. Take a dry cloth and wipe off the clear side of the CD from the inside ring to the outside edge.
  2. Check the CD for deep scratches. If it is damaged, you may have to obtain a replacement disc by calling 1-866-236-6980.
  3. If the CD looks fine, try running the program again.

For Problems in Running the Program:

  1. Make sure that VIRTUAL MEMORY is ON. (Turn it on in Memory Control Panel and restart computer).
  2. Close all other applications running in the background. This can be done by going to the Finder menu in the upper right hand corner and clicking and holding down the mouse button. The only application running should be FINDER.
  3. Make sure that RAM DISK is off. (Turn it off in Memory Control Panel and restart computer).
  4. If installed, make sure that AT EASE is off. (Disable it in StartUp).
  5. If installed, make sure that RAM DOUBLER is off. (Turn it off or disable the extension).
  6. If you are using the OS 10 operating system, try running Publix Preschool Pals in classic mode.

Reset the Game

7. TO SIGN-IN

Click on the entry field at the top left of your screen. Type in your name. When you’re finished, click GO or hit the return key to begin. To resume a saved game, find your name on the list and click on it. Your name will appear in the text entry field. Click GO to begin.



8. A BIRD'S EYE VIEW OF THE PALS' HOMETOWN

Signing in takes you to an aerial view of the Pals' hometown, with all the places your children can visit – the fire station, library, art studio, playground, homes and Publix. It's here that your children choose a game to play. And really, it couldn't be easier. Rolling over the different areas prompts the Pal in that area to pop up. Plato, who's floating nearby in a hot air balloon, says to click the Pal to enter the game. To exit a game and return to this Hometown screen, click on the back arrow in the left hand corner of the page.



9. CHANGING LEVELS OF DIFFICULTY

Each game except Otis's Coloring Book has three levels. The games automatically start on the first level. To switch to another level, click on the "1 - 2 - 3" icon at the bottom of the page. This brings up the level-changing screen. The level you're on is highlighted at the top of the page. Simply click on the number of the level you want. Then click yes, and the game resets to that level. Or click no, and the game goes back to the current level. To exit the screen and return to the game, click on the back arrow button in the left hand corner of the page.



10. ABOUT THE COINS

Notice the "Coin" icon at the bottom of the page. It's in the bottom right corner. What's this all about? Simple. Your children earn coins for playing the games. Level 1, bronze coins. Each coin is made up of three pieces. They must play the game three times to earn a whole bronze coin. Level 2, silver. Each coin is made up of four pieces. Your children must play the game four times to earn a whole silver coin. Level 3, gold. Each coin is made up of six pieces. Your children must play the game six times to earn a whole gold coin. When they've earned a coin, they're taken to a screen where Plato congratulates them and drops their coin in a slot, which initiates the coin's fascinating journey to the bank. The "Coin" icon at the bottom of the page shows how many pieces of the coin your children have collected. To exit the coin bank screen and return to the game, click on the back arrow button in the bottom center of the page. There's one exception to all this. In the Kitty & Kimmy game, your children will earn a piece of a coin each time they put one item, or multiples of one item on higher levels, into their shopping baskets.



11. SKIP OPTION

If you want to skip the introduction to any section – general intro to the CD, to each character or the rewards section, just press the space bar.



GAMES AND HELP



12. BARON'S SAFETY MEMORY

Skills learned: Safety object identification, object (shape and color) matching, short-term memory.

What's a St. Bernard doing in a firehouse? Your children find out soon enough as Baron asks them to match objects related to safety. That's right, this is an always-popular match game. Bandage to bandage. Police hat to hat. Have your children click around the page for some surprises. To switch to another level, click on the "1 - 2 - 3" icon at the bottom of the page.



13. BIXBY'S ALPHABETICAL BOOK FINDER

Skills learned: Letter recognition, alphabet, phonics - beginning and lower level, uppercase and lowercase letter matching.

Bixby is everybody's favorite bookworm. Today, he welcomes your children into the town library, where every book's title is a different letter. He needs their help filing and organizing. If your children don't know the alphabet yet, they will when Bixby gets finished with them. Have your children click around the page for some surprises. To switch to another level, click on the "1 - 2 - 3" icon at the bottom of the page.



14. CHAZ'S HIDE AND SEEK

Skills learned: Spatial visual reference, cause and effect.

It's a gorgeous Saturday. Chaz The Cheetah's parents have taken him to the park. And he's feeling playful. So he's decided a game of hide and seek with your children is just the thing. Only rule is, he wants to be the one hiding. Your children, of course, are the ones doing the seeking. All they have to do is click the arrow on the object they think Chaz is hiding behind. Have them click around the page for some surprises. To switch to another level, click on the "1 - 2 - 3" icon at the bottom of the page.



15. HENNA'S FOOD PREPARATION

Skills learned: Food and food group identification, following directions.

Sweet, warm and wonderful Henna The Hen. She's everybody's favorite cook. But today, she needs a little help planning her meals and this is where your children come in. She tells them what she needs. And they click on the right item to make it hop onto her plate. Have them click around the page for some surprises. To switch to another level, click on the "1 - 2 - 3" icon at the bottom of the page.



16. KITTY AND KIMMY'S TRIP TO PUBLIX

Skills learned: Counting, food and food group identification.

Kitty Kangaroo and her daughter Kimmy invite your children to go grocery shopping with them. It's a visual smorgasbord as they move from one department to the next filling their cart with everything on their list. Actually, on this trip they want your children to do the actual plucking off the shelves. And that's ok, cause they learn some helpful lessons. Have them click around the page for some surprises. To switch to another level, click on the "1 - 2 - 3" icon at the bottom of the page.



17. OTIS'S COLORING BOOK

Skills learned: Color recognition, food familiarity, food group recognition.

Otis The Octopus is an art teacher, only he has a few more arms than the ones your children are probably used to. And in those arms he has crayons, paint sprayers, an eraser and stickers of lips and reading glasses – all sorts of fun facial accessories. Children participate by choosing what pictures to color, then going wild with them.



18. PEARL'S GROCERY PUT-AWAY

Skills learned: Food identification, kitchen familiarity, logic.

Pearl the Polar Bear has just returned to her really cool house from grocery shopping at Publix and needs your children's help putting everything away where it belongs. Steak, vegetables, frozen goods, items for the freezer, others for the fridge. Your children click on items and drag them where they think they belong. Have them click around the page for some surprises. To switch to another level, click on the "1 - 2 - 3" icon at the bottom of the page.



19. INTERACTIVE DISCOVERIES

On many pages, there are surprises to be discovered. Encourage your children to click their mouse randomly around the screen and see what happens. Something silly may just pop up out of nowhere.



20. GET HELP

Feeling stuck while you're playing one of the games? Click on the image of Plato between the 1-2-3 and the coin icons and get help fast.
